I remember the first time I saw a computer, I was 11 or 12. It was at the Florida State Fair and it had 10 math questions. I had to enter the answer for each one. One question had a zero in the answer and I pressed the letter 'O'. There was a person who told me I needed to press the other 'O' because it would not work. This piqued my curiosity and I asked them if I can push it anyway. They told me I can but I would get the question wrong. So I proceeded and they were correct. But I was more content knowing what happens getting a 90 then I would've getting 100.


During high school I would go to the computer lab and I taught myself basic. Then I took a computer class and I did learn some things in the class but t was the easiest A I ever received. Due to my prior experirence I was able to help many of the students with their labs. Self-teaching became a hallmark in my career.


Being unable to find a job in computers with nothing to show for it, no work experience, no degree and no certificate I with to a 6 month class which taught JCL, machine language and COBOL. This allowed me to get a job as a computer operator. There was a rich learning experience. I learned Control-O which is automation software for the mainframe. I wrote code to shut down the system in 5 minutes when it would usually take the operator about a half an hour. A couple of times they told me they raced through it and got it down between 12 and 15 minutes. It reminded me of the story of John Henry. I also learned REXX and ISPF panels, which looked like windows on the dumb terminals, albeit without much of the functionality like moving a window. It was here where I first learned VB.
